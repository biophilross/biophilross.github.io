I am a protein biochemist by training, but a biologist with broad interests that range from the atomic to organismal scale in principle. I have a B.S. in Bioengineering from Binghamton University and a Ph.D. in Genetics from the University of Chicago.

As a computational biologist in the lab of Manuel Llin√°s, I contributed to studies of transcriptional regulation in Plasmodium falciparum, which inspired me pursue a Ph.D. in order to learn more about life at the molecular scale.

As a Ph.D. student in the lab of Erin Adams, I utilized methods in protein biochemistry and structural biology to understand how archaic and non-classical HLA molecules “present” antigens to innate immune receptors.
